# PoolApp Demo Script - 5-Minute Walkthrough

**Goal:** Convert pool service owner into paying customer in 15 minutes or less

**Vibe:** Confident, efficient, value-focused. Not salesy.

---

## Pre-Demo Prep

**Before the call, know their situation:**
- Company name
- Fleet size (number of routes/technicians)
- Current routing method
- Biggest pain point (from outreach/research)
- Average revenue per job

**Have these ready:**
- PoolApp loaded in browser (dashboard open)
- Test data showing impressive numbers
- Calculator open for quick ROI

---

## Demo Script

### 1. Opening (1 minute)

**You:** "Hey [Name], thanks for hopping on the call. I've got PoolApp pulled up here and ready to walk you through it."

**If they haven't seen it yet:** "Before we dive in, have you seen PoolApp before or is this your first time looking at it?"

**Then set agenda:** "Here's what I want to show you today - this should only take about 15 minutes. I'll walk through how PoolApp can save you time and money on routes, and if anything's interesting, we can go deeper. Sound good?"

**Get their time:** "Do you have 15 minutes now, or should we schedule a follow-up?"

---

### 2. The Problem We Solve (2 minutes)

**Transition:** "So, I talked to a lot of pool service owners in [City], and the number one thing I hear is route planning takes way too long."

**Paint the picture:** 
- Most owners spend 2-3 hours every night planning routes
- They're manually optimizing - "which customer goes after which"
- Using Excel, Route4Me, or "just know it by heart"

**Connect to their reality:** "How are you handling route planning right now? Are you using spreadsheets, Route4Me, something else?"

**Listen for their answer** - They'll tell you. Acknowledge what they're doing.

**Pivot to solution:** "That's exactly what PoolApp solves. Our AI finds the fastest route based on traffic, job priority, technician availability - all in one click. Instead of spending 2 hours tonight, you spend 5 minutes."

**Key point:** "We typically see companies save 500+ hours per year just on route planning. That's time you could be billing more jobs."

**Check interest:** "Does that resonate with you? Are you tired of losing evenings to spreadsheets?"

---

### 3. The Solution - Live Walkthrough (5 minutes)

**Transition:** "Let me show you how it works. I'm on the dashboard right now - let me walk through it."

**Navigation:** "I'll move to the Schedule page so you can see how route planning works, then we'll look at revenue tracking and alerts."

**Do it:** Navigate to /schedule

**Explain what they're seeing:** "So here on the Schedule page - you're seeing the weekly view. Each row represents a technician, and each cell shows their jobs for the day."

**Point out key features:**

1. **Auto-Optimize Button:** "See that 'Auto-Optimize' button up top? That's our one-click route optimization. You click it, our AI reorders the entire day based on traffic patterns, technician schedules, and job priority. Takes seconds instead of hours."

2. **Revenue Tracking:** "Scrolling down to the dashboard - see 'Today's Revenue' and 'This Month'? That's real-time. Every job shows up instantly. No more lost jobs, no more guessing what you're making."

3. **Chemistry Alerts:** "See that 'Alerts' section on dashboard? 'pH high at Johnson - prevented $500 callback'? That's our chemistry alert system. We catch issues before they become expensive callbacks. You prevent the problem before it happens."

4. **Technician Tracking:** "Back to schedule - each technician card shows their progress for the day. 6/8 pools. You can see who's running behind and auto-adjust their route on the fly."

**Ask for reaction:** "That's the core of it - route planning, revenue tracking, chemistry alerts, technician management, all in one place."

**Pause for impact:** "I want you to think about what that means for your operation. How much time would you save? How many callbacks would you prevent? What's that worth to you?"

---

### 4. ROI - The Money Talk (3 minutes)

**Acknowledge:** "I know you're thinking about the math. Let me break it down with real numbers from companies I've worked with."

**Use their inputs (ask):** "Roughly how many routes are you running right now? And what's your average revenue per service?"

**If they give numbers, do quick math:** "So with [X] routes and an average of $[Y] per job, you're probably doing around $[calc] per month. If PoolApp saves you even 10% on route planning alone, that's $[savings] in your pocket annually."

**Compare to alternatives:** "Route4Me would cost you $150/month plus per-driver fees. PoolApp is $39-$79/month. No per-driver charges, no setup fees, you're up and running in 5 minutes."

**The question:** "At $39/month, if PoolApp saves you 2 hours of planning time every night, doesn't it pay for itself in about a month? And that's before we even talk about the other benefits like callbacks and revenue tracking."

---

### 5. Objections & Closing (3 minutes)

**Pre-empt common objections:**

**Objection 1: "We're happy with our current process."**

Response: "Totally get it. I'm not trying to sell you something you don't need. Here's what I'd suggest - take PoolApp for a spin. Setup takes 5 minutes. Try it for one week on your routes. If you're saving 2+ hours per night, you'll know it within a week. If you like it, great. If not, cancel anytime. You've got nothing to lose."

**Objection 2: "This sounds expensive - we don't have budget for software right now."**

Response: "I hear you. Let me ask - how much is your time worth? If your routes take 2 hours to plan and you're billing say $100/hour for your time, that's $200/night you're spending on planning. PoolApp would cut that to 5 minutes. You'd save $185/night. That's over $5,000 a year. Even if you only run it seasonally, it pays for itself. What's your time worth - $5,000 or $185/night?"

**Objection 3: "I need to talk to my partner/boss before making decisions."**

Response: "Makes perfect sense. PoolApp is a new tool, and you should absolutely involve your team. How about this - I can send you a free 30-day trial account. Your team can use it, you can see exactly how it works, no risk. At the end of 30 days, you'll have data to make a decision. Sound fair?"

**Objection 4: "We use a custom system that works fine."**

Response: "Good to hear. Custom systems can work great until you hit a certain scale. But I'll ask - are you growing? Adding more routes, hiring more techs? Because at that point, spreadsheets and manual planning really start holding you back. PoolApp scales with you - whether you have 5 routes or 50, it's $39-$79/month. No per-driver seat charges. You're not paying per tech."

---

### 6. The Close (1 minute)

**Assess the call:** "Before we wrap up, I want to make sure I answered everything. Was there anything specific about route planning or revenue tracking that you wanted to see?"

**Get commitment to next step:**

**If they're interested:** "Great! I'd love to set you up with an account. Would you like to do that now? I can get you created in 2 minutes and you'll have full access immediately."

**If they're not interested but want more info:** "No worries at all. Can I email you some materials - maybe the ROI calculator showing your specific numbers, or a comparison sheet vs Route4Me?"

**If they need time:** "Totally understand. How about I email you some resources to review with your team, and we can reconnect in a week or two? Does that work?"

**Final close:** "Appreciate your time today, [Name]. We'll be in touch."

---

## Tips for Success

**Energy:** Keep it high but grounded. You know this stuff inside and out.

**Listen more than you talk:** The best demos feel like conversations, not presentations.

**Use their data:** When showing the dashboard, reference their specific numbers if you know them.

**Be ready for objections:** They'll ask questions. That's buying behavior. It's good - it means they're interested.

**Speed:** If they're clearly interested, move to setup quickly. If they're hesitant, slow down and address concerns.

**Follow up immediately:** Send account details within 5 minutes of the call while it's fresh.
